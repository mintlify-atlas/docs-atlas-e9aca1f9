## Best Practices for Applications

### Apply Early and Often

<Info>
  Many internship programs operate on a rolling basis - they review applications as they come in and may close the position once they find strong candidates. Apply as soon as you find a relevant opportunity!
</Info>

### Tailor Your Materials

While Simplify can help with basic information, customize these elements for each application:

- **Cover letter** - Reference specific projects or technologies the company uses
- **Resume bullet points** - Highlight experiences relevant to the role
- **Portfolio/GitHub** - Ensure your best work is visible and well-documented

### Track Your Applications

Keep a spreadsheet or use a tool to track:
- Company name and position
- Date applied
- Application status
- Interview dates
- Follow-up tasks

## Understanding Position Requirements

### Special Indicators to Watch For

When browsing listings, pay attention to these indicators:

- 🎓 **Advanced degree required** - These positions specifically require or strongly prefer Master's, MBA, or PhD candidates
- 🛂 **Does NOT offer sponsorship** - You must already have work authorization
- 🇺🇸 **Requires U.S. Citizenship** - Typically for defense or government-related positions
- 🔥 **FAANG+ company** - Highly competitive positions at top-tier tech companies

### Location Considerations

Many internships now offer flexible location options:
- **Hybrid** - Mix of remote and in-office work
- **Remote** - Fully remote from anywhere in the US/Canada
- **Multiple locations** - You may have a choice of offices

## The Interview Process

### Common Interview Stages

1. **Phone Screen** - 30-minute call with recruiter or hiring manager
2. **Technical Assessment** - Online coding challenge or take-home project
3. **Technical Interviews** - 1-3 rounds of coding and system design questions
4. **Behavioral Interview** - Questions about teamwork, problem-solving, and past experiences
5. **Final Round** - Often includes meeting with senior engineers or managers
