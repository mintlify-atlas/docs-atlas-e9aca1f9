## Finding the Right Off-Season Internship

### By Start Date

**Fall 2025 (Starting Aug/Sept)**:
- Best for students graduating in December
- Co-op programs aligned with Fall semester
- Gap semester before senior year

**Winter 2026 (Starting Jan)**:
- Aligned with Winter/Spring semester
- Popular for co-op rotations
- Students on semester break

**Spring 2026 (Starting Mar/Apr)**:
- May extend into summer
- Good for graduating seniors
- Post-graduation internships before full-time

### By Company Type

**Large Tech Companies**:
- Structured co-op programs
- Well-defined start/end dates
- Often multiple locations
- Examples: Oracle, Intel, Boeing, Fidelity

**Startups & Scale-ups**:
- More flexible start dates
- Longer-term projects
- Faster-paced environment
- Examples: Mercury, Clipboard Health, Prolaio

**Defense & Aerospace**:
- Often require U.S. citizenship 🇺🇸
- Longer-term co-ops (6+ months)
- Significant security clearances
- Examples: Boeing, General Dynamics, Leidos

## Why Choose Off-Season?

### Advantages

1. **Less competition** - Fewer students apply
2. **Better mentorship** - Smaller intern cohorts
3. **Project ownership** - Longer projects with more impact
4. **Flexible timing** - Fits co-op programs and gap semesters
5. **Higher conversion rates** - Better chance of return offers

### Ideal For

- **Co-op students** - Required work terms
- **Gap semester** - Taking time off school
- **December graduates** - Start working early
- **Part-time students** - Balancing work and school
- **Graduate students** - More flexible schedules
- **Career changers** - Getting experience while studying

## Academic Considerations

### Working with Your School

Before accepting an off-season internship:

1. **Check graduation requirements** - Will this delay graduation?
2. **Coordinate with advisors** - Get co-op credit if available
3. **Financial aid** - How does taking time off affect aid?
4. **Housing** - Can you sublease? Does company provide housing?
5. **Course sequence** - Will you miss prerequisite courses?

### Co-op Programs

If your school has a formal co-op program:
- Register the internship through your co-op office
- May receive academic credit
- Some companies prefer official co-op students
- Schools maintain relationships with companies

<Note>
  **Pro tip**: Even if your school doesn't have a formal co-op program, you can often get internship credit through independent study or practicum courses.
</Note>

## International Students

### Work Authorization

**F-1 Students** can work off-season through:
- **CPT (Curricular Practical Training)** - Must be part of curriculum
- **OPT (Optional Practical Training)** - After graduation or between terms

**Important**:
- 🛂 indicates companies that don't sponsor visas
- Check with your international student office before applying
- Some off-season positions are more flexible with start dates for visa processing
