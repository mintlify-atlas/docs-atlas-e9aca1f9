## What is Product Management?

Product managers sit at the intersection of business, technology, and user experience. They:

- Define product vision and strategy
- Prioritize features and roadmap items
- Work with engineering teams to build products
- Analyze user data and feedback
- Coordinate cross-functional teams
- Launch and iterate on products

## Skills for PM Internships

### Technical Skills

While not always required, these skills strengthen PM applications:

- **Basic coding** - Understanding of software development
- **SQL** - Querying databases for product analytics
- **Data analysis** - Excel, Python, R for analyzing metrics
- **Analytics tools** - Familiarity with Mixpanel, Amplitude, Google Analytics
- **Design tools** - Figma, Sketch for working with designers

### Soft Skills

Critical skills for product managers:

- **Communication** - Explaining complex ideas to diverse audiences
- **Prioritization** - Making tradeoffs between competing priorities
- **User empathy** - Understanding customer needs and pain points
- **Analytical thinking** - Data-driven decision making
- **Leadership** - Influencing without authority
- **Collaboration** - Working across engineering, design, sales, marketing

## Interview Process

### Common PM Interview Stages

1. **Phone Screen** - 30-minute call with recruiter
2. **PM Case Study** - Product design, strategy, or analytics case
3. **Behavioral Interviews** - Past experiences and situational questions
4. **Product Sense** - How would you improve product X?
5. **Technical Assessment** - SQL, coding, or analytical problem (for technical PM roles)
6. **Final Round** - Meet the team and leadership

### Typical PM Interview Questions

<Info>
  **Product Design**: "Design a product for X users"
  
  **Strategy**: "How would you prioritize these features?"
  
  **Metrics**: "What metrics would you track for Y product?"
  
  **Root Cause Analysis**: "Usage of feature Z dropped 20%, why?"
  
  **Go-to-Market**: "How would you launch this new product?"
</Info>

## Application Tips

### Build Your PM Portfolio

- **Side projects** - Build and launch your own products
- **Product teardowns** - Write analyses of products you love/hate
- **Case studies** - Document how you solved problems
- **Product blog** - Share your thinking on product decisions
- **PM competitions** - Participate in product case competitions

### Strengthen Your Application

1. **Show impact** - Quantify results in your resume
2. **Tell stories** - Use the STAR method for behavioral questions
3. **Be curious** - Research the company's products deeply
4. **Network** - Connect with PMs at target companies
5. **Get referrals** - Internal referrals significantly improve your chances

<Note>
  **Pro tip**: Before interviewing, use the company's products extensively. PMs are expected to be power users who deeply understand the product experience.
</Note>
